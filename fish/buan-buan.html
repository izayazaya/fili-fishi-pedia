Buan-buan

The Buan-buan is a small fish species commonly found in the Philippines, valued for its availability in local markets and use in traditional dishes.

Common Name: Buan-buan

Scientific Name: Stolephorus spp.

Size: 6 cm - 10 cm

Weight: 0.005 kg - 0.015 kg

Season: Year-round peaking in March - June

Market Price(kg): PHP 100 - 300

The Buan-buan is commonly found in the coastal and brackish waters of the Philippines, particularly in estuaries, mangrove areas, and shallow coastal zones. It thrives in warm, shallow waters and often schools in large numbers, making it abundant in these areas.

The Buan-buan plays an important role in the fishing industry, particularly in the production of fermented products such as bagoong (fermented fish paste) and alamang (fermented shrimp paste). These products are staples in Filipino cuisine, commonly used as condiments or ingredients in various dishes like sinigang (sour soup) or ginataan (coconut milk-based dishes). The small size and abundance of Buan-buan make it a vital resource for local fishermen, supporting both small-scale fisheries and the commercial fish industry. The demand for this fish, especially in its processed form, contributes significantly to the Philippines' seafood market and supports local economies in coastal communities.
