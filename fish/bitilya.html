Bitilya

The Bitalya is a small but important fish species in the Philippines, prized for its culinary uses and availability in local markets.

Common Name: Bitilya

Scientific Name: Stolephorus spp.

Size: 7 cm - 10 cm

Weight: 5 g - 10 g

Season: Year-round peaking in March - June

Market Price(kg): PHP 380 - 680

The Bitalya is commonly found in the coastal and brackish waters of the Philippines, particularly in estuaries, mangrove areas, and shallow coastal zones. It thrives in warm, shallow water and is often found in large schools.

The Bitalya plays an essential role in the Filipino fishing industry, particularly in the production of fermented products such as bagoong (fish paste) and alamang (fermented shrimp paste). These products are staples in Filipino cuisine, used as condiments or ingredients in various dishes. The small size and abundance of Bitalya make it a crucial resource for local fishermen, providing a source of income and protein for many coastal communities. Due to its high demand in both local markets and for export, it supports both small-scale fisheries and larger commercial operations in the Philippines.
