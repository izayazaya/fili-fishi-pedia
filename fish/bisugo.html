Bisugo

The Bisugo is a highly regarded fish species in the Philippines, prized for its flavor and versatility in local cuisine.

Common Name: Bisugo

Scientific Name: Nemipterus nematophorus

Size: 20 cm - 30 cm

Weight: 0.1 kg - 0.25 kg

Season: Year-round peaking in March - June

Market Price(kg): PHP 500 - 550

The Bisugo is commonly found in the coastal waters of the Philippines, particularly in shallow and deeper areas near reefs, sandy bottoms, and offshore islands. It is distributed throughout the archipelago, including the Visayan Sea, Sulu Sea, and other coastal regions.

The Bisugo is commercially important in the Philippine fishing industry due to its high demand and versatile culinary uses. Known for its delicate, mild flavor and tender white meat, Bisugo is often grilled, fried, or used in soups and stews like sinigang. It is also processed for export, including fresh, dried, and frozen forms. The fish is popular in Filipino households and restaurants, contributing significantly to local markets, especially in coastal communities where it supports small-scale fisheries. With its relatively high market value, Bisugo is also a favored choice for export, bringing economic benefits to the Philippine seafood industry.
