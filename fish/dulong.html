Dulong

Common Name: Dulong

Scientific Name: Hypseleotris spp.

Size: 5 cm - 10 cm

Weight: 5 g - 10 g

Season: Year-round peaking in June - September

Market Price(kg): PHP 200 - 250

Dulong is commonly found in brackish and freshwater environments in the Philippines, particularly in rivers, ponds, lakes, and estuaries. It is abundant in areas like the coastal regions of Luzon, Visayas, and Mindanao, especially in places with shallow, muddy waters.

Dulong (Hypseleotris spp.) is a small but commercially significant fish in the Philippines, valued for its delicate and translucent flesh. It is usually sold fresh or dried and is widely used in local dishes, such as soups, stews, and fish paste (bagoong). Dulong is often harvested in large quantities using small nets or traps by local fishermen, and it is particularly popular in rural markets. The fish is prized for its affordability and nutritional value, providing a source of protein in many Filipino households. Dulong's small size makes it an easy addition to various culinary preparations, and it is also used to make salted or fermented products. The fish plays an essential role in both local consumption and small-scale commercial fisheries across the Philippines.
