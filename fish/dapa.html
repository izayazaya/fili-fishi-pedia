Dapa

Common Name: Dapa

Scientific Name: Pleuronectidae

Size: 15 cm - 50 cm

Weight: 0.2 kg - 1 kg

Season: Year-round peaking in June - November

Market Price(kg): PHP 700 - 750

Dapa is found in shallow coastal waters, estuaries, and sandy or muddy seabeds throughout the Philippines. It is particularly abundant in areas like Manila Bay, Visayan Sea, and coastal regions near Mindoro and Palawan. Its bottom-dwelling nature makes it suitable for trawl and net fishing.

Dapa (flounder) is an economically significant fish in the Philippines, prized for its delicate, white meat and mild flavor. It is widely consumed fresh or dried and is used in various Filipino dishes such as soups, stews, or fried preparations. Dapa is commonly caught by artisanal and commercial fishers using nets and bottom trawls. Its adaptability to different cooking methods and year-round availability make it a staple in local wet markets. Efforts to maintain sustainable harvesting practices and habitat conservation are important to ensure the continued abundance of this valuable fish.
