Halaan

Halaan is a type of clam commonly found in the coastal waters of the Philippines. It is known for its distinct shape and is highly prized for its sweet, tender meat. These clams thrive in sandy or muddy areas in shallow waters.

Common Name: Halaan

Scientific Name: Anadara antiquata

Size: 10 cm - 12 cm

Weight: 0.1 kg - 0.2 kg

Season: Year-round peaking in November to April

Market Price(kg): PHP 240 - 340

Halaan can be found in various coastal regions across the Philippines, especially in areas like Pangasinan, Bataan, and other provinces with shallow bays or beaches. They are often harvested from sandy or muddy seabeds in the tidal flats or near mangrove areas.

Halaan is a significant part of the Philippine seafood market, often sold fresh, shelled, or cooked in various local dishes. It is particularly popular in soups, stews, and as a side dish in Filipino cuisine, known for its tender meat and subtle sweetness. Commercially, halaan is typically harvested by local fishermen and shellfish gatherers, either by hand or with the use of specialized tools, making it a vital part of the coastal economy. It is consumed not only locally but also exported to other regions, contributing to the livelihood of many Filipino families in coastal areas.
