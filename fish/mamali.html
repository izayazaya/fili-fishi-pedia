Mamali

Common Name: Mamali

Scientific Name: Sphyraena spp.

Size: 1 m - 1.5 m

Weight: 5 kgs - 20 kgs

Season: Year-round peaking in November - April

Market Price(kg): PHP 120 - 250

The Mamali, or barracuda, can be found in coastal waters, particularly around coral reefs, drop-offs, and island shores in the Philippines. It is commonly encountered in both the Visayan Sea and areas in Palawan, Mindanao, and northern Luzon, preferring warm, shallow waters, though it can also be found deeper at times.

The Mamali, or barracuda, is a commercially important fish in the Philippines, known for its fast, predatory nature and firm, white flesh. It is highly sought after for its culinary value, particularly for grilling and frying. The fish is often caught using hooks or trolling methods, and it plays a crucial role in the local fishing industry, contributing to both small-scale and larger commercial fisheries. While not as abundant as some other fish species, barracuda is valued for its unique taste and texture, often appearing in high-end markets and restaurants. However, due to the presence of toxins like ciguatera in some larger specimens, care is taken when selling and consuming it.
