Alupihang-dagat

The Alupihang-dagat, commonly known as the mantis shrimp, is a marine crustacean found in Philippine waters. Known for their vibrant colors and strong predatory abilities, mantis shrimp are highly valued in local cuisine. They are agile hunters that use their specialized appendages to smash or spear prey, making them one of the most fascinating marine species.

Common Name: Alupihang-dagat

Scientific Name: Odontodactylus scyllarus

Size: 10 cm - 30 cm

Weight: Can weigh up to 0.5 kg

Season: Available year-round

Market Price(kg): PHP 250 - 300

The Alupihang-dagat is commonly found in shallow tropical and subtropical waters, particularly in sandy or muddy seabeds, coral reefs, and mangroves. Prime locations include Palawan, Visayas, and the Mindoro Strait.

Alupihang-dagat is a sought-after seafood delicacy in the Philippines, appreciated for its sweet, lobster-like meat. It is primarily harvested by small-scale fishermen and sold fresh or frozen in local markets. While not a staple in commercial aquaculture, it has a growing demand in gourmet cuisine and export markets due to its exotic appeal and nutritional value. Efforts are being made to sustainably manage its populations to prevent overfishing.
