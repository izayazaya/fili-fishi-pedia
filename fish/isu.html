Isu

The fish commonly known as "Isu" in the Philippines is identified scientifically as Parupeneus heptolepis, commonly known as the Seven-spot red snapper. It is typically found in shallow, coastal waters of the Philippines and other parts of the Indo-Pacific region.

Common Name: Isu

Scientific Name: Parupeneus heptolepis

Size: 25 cm - 35 cm

Weight: 0.5 kg - 1.5 kg

Season: Year-round

Market Price(kg): PHP 100 - 400

The Isu fish can be found in various coastal areas of the Philippines, particularly in the Visayas and Mindanao regions. It thrives in coral reefs and sandy or muddy bottoms of coastal zones. It is most abundant during the dry season when fishing activities peak.

Commercially, Isu is highly valued for its firm, white flesh and mild flavor. It is a popular choice in local markets and is often sold fresh or dried. The fish is typically utilized in a wide array of Filipino dishes, such as grilled or fried preparations, and is a favorite in festive meals. Due to its taste and accessibility, the Isu has a consistent presence in local fisheries, contributing significantly to the livelihood of coastal communities and the seafood trade in the Philippines.
