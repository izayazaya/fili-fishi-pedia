Burara

The Burara is a small fish species found in the Philippines, commonly used in local cuisine and fishing industries.

Common Name: Burara

Scientific Name: Stolephorus spp.

Size: 6 cm - 8 cm

Weight: 0.005 kg - 0.01 kg

Season: Year-round peaking in March - June

Market Price(kg): PHP 150 - 240

The Burara is commonly found in the coastal and brackish waters of the Philippines, especially in mangrove areas, estuaries, and shallow coastal zones. It thrives in warm, shallow water and is often found in schools, making it easy to catch in large quantities.

The Burara plays an important role in the Philippine fishing industry, particularly in the production of bagoong (fermented fish paste) and alamang (fermented shrimp paste). These products are staples in Filipino cuisine, used as seasonings or condiments in a variety of traditional dishes. The small size and abundance of the Burara make it a valuable resource for local fishermen, providing income for coastal communities. The fish is harvested in large quantities, supporting both local consumption and commercial markets. Due to its role in the production of popular fermented products, the Burara is integral to the Philippine seafood industry.
