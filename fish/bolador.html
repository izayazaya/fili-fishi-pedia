Bolador

Common Name: Bolador

Scientific Name: Decapterus macrosoma

Size: 15 cm - 20 cm

Weight: 100 g - 150 g

Season: Year-round peaking in June - September

Market Price(kg): PHP 100 - 200

Bolador is commonly found in coastal and offshore waters across the Philippines, particularly in areas such as the Visayan Sea, Sulu Sea, and the waters surrounding Palawan and Mindoro. It thrives in schools near the surface, making it accessible to fishermen.

The Bolador, also known as the shortfin scad, is a commercially significant fish in the Philippines, widely consumed and traded in local markets. It is valued for its affordability, tender flesh, and mild flavor, making it a favorite among households. Bolador is typically prepared as fried, grilled, or dried (daing), contributing to its versatility. This fish is a primary source of income for small-scale and commercial fishers, particularly during its peak season when large schools migrate through Philippine waters. Its role in the food industry and its contribution to coastal livelihoods highlight its importance in the local economy.
