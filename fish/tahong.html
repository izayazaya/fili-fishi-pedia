Tahong

Tahong, commonly known as mussel, is a type of shellfish that thrives in coastal areas. It is popular in many seafood dishes and is commonly harvested in the Philippines, where it is often cultivated in brackish waters.

Common Name: Tahong

Scientific Name: Perna viridis

Size: 7 cm - 10 cm

Weight: 0.1 kg - 0.2 kg

Season: Year-round peaking in November to March

Market Price(kg): PHP 120 - 200

Tahong is found in many coastal areas in the Philippines, particularly in the provinces of Bataan, Cavite, and Batangas, where they are farmed in coastal waters, particularly in shallow, brackish environments.

Tahong is a commercially important shellfish in the Philippines, both as a food source and for its role in aquaculture. Mussels are widely cultivated on farms in regions with suitable water conditions, where they grow quickly in the nutrient-rich waters. The shells are harvested, cleaned, and often sold fresh, frozen, or in processed forms like canned mussels. In addition to their culinary uses, mussels are valued for their health benefits, rich in protein, vitamins, and minerals. The shellfish is integral to the local economy, particularly in coastal communities, where it provides income for fishermen and contributes to the seafood industry's supply chain.
