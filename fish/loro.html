Loro

The Loro fish, found in the Philippines, is scientifically known as Siganus canaliculatus, also called the Yellowtail Rabbitfish.

Common Name: Loro

Scientific Name: Siganus canaliculatus

Size: 30 cm - 40 cm

Weight: 0.5 kg - 1 kg

Season: Year-round peaking in November - April

Market Price(kg): PHP 200 - 500

Loro is commonly found in shallow coastal waters, particularly in areas with seagrass beds, coral reefs, and lagoons throughout the Philippines. It is abundant in regions such as the Visayas, Mindanao, and parts of Luzon, where it thrives in warm, tropical waters.

Commercially, the Loro fish is valued for its tender and flavorful meat, making it a popular choice in Filipino cuisine. It is often used in dishes like sinigang (sour soup), grilled preparations, or kinilaw (raw fish salad). The fish is sold fresh in local markets and is also farmed in certain coastal areas due to its adaptability and fast growth. The Loro plays a key role in both local consumption and the seafood industry in the Philippines, supporting small-scale fisheries and fish farming, especially in coastal communities where it provides a vital source of income. The fish is also exported, contributing to the country's seafood trade.
