Malaga

The Malaga fish, found in the Philippines, is scientifically known as Cynoglossus lingua, also known as the Tongue Sole.

Common Name: Malaga

Scientific Name: Cynoglossus lingua

Size: 20 cm - 30 cm

Weight: 0.2 kg - 0.5 kg

Season: Year-round peaking in November - May

Market Price(kg): PHP 150 - 300

The Malaga fish is found in the coastal waters of the Philippines, especially in sandy or muddy bottoms in shallow waters, often near estuaries and river mouths. It is commonly found in areas like the Visayas, Mindanao, and parts of Luzon.

Commercially, the Malaga fish is valued for its mild, sweet flavor and tender, delicate flesh. It is often used in Filipino cuisine for dishes like sinigang (sour soup), paksiw (fish cooked in vinegar), or simply fried. It is available in local markets, usually fresh or salted, and is particularly popular in coastal communities. Due to its delicate texture, Malaga is often considered a specialty fish. While not as widely distributed as some other fish species, it remains a significant part of the local seafood trade, providing a steady source of income for small-scale fishermen in certain regions. The fish's mild flavor and availability in local markets also make it a favorite in home cooking.
