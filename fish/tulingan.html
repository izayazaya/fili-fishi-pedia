Tulingan

The Tulingan fish, found in the Philippines, is scientifically known as Auxis thazard, commonly referred to as the Frigate Tuna.

Common Name: Tulingan

Scientific Name: Auxis thazard

Size: 40 cm - 60 cm

Weight: 2 kg - 5 kg

Season: Year-round peaking in May - October

Market Price(kg): PHP 150 - 300

The Tulingan fish is found in the coastal waters of the Philippines, particularly in the Visayas, Mindanao, and parts of Luzon. It is a migratory species that is commonly found in offshore waters and is often caught by commercial fishing boats using trolling and long-line methods.

Commercially, the Tulingan is highly valued for its firm, meaty flesh and is a popular fish in Filipino cuisine. It is often used in dishes like sinigang (sour soup), kilawin (raw fish marinated in vinegar), and paksiw (fish cooked in vinegar). Tulingan is also commonly canned or preserved through smoking, drying, or salting for longer shelf life. It is sold fresh in local markets and is especially popular in coastal provinces where it is abundant. The fish is not as widely known as other tuna species but has a strong demand in the local market due to its affordability and versatility in cooking. The Tulingan fish is also an important export product, contributing to the Philippine fishing industry. Sustainable management of Tulingan fisheries is necessary to prevent overfishing and ensure the long-term viability of the species in the wild.
