Susô

Susô is a type of marine mollusk commonly known for its spiral-shaped shell. It is a popular delicacy in various coastal regions and is prized for its tender meat.

Common Name: Susô

Scientific Name: Trochus niloticus

Size: 5 cm - 7 cm

Weight: 0.1 kg - 0.2 kg

Season: Year-round peaking in May to September

Market Price(kg): PHP 150 - 300

Susô is typically found in the shallow waters of the Philippines' coral reefs, particularly around islands such as Palawan, Visayas, and parts of Mindanao.

Susô, or trochus, is commercially valued for its meat and its shell. The meat is commonly harvested for food, often used in soups, stews, and other Filipino seafood dishes. Its shells are also prized for crafting jewelry, ornaments, and souvenirs, contributing to local handicrafts. Due to its commercial potential, Susô is a significant species for small-scale coastal fisheries in the Philippines, although overharvesting is a concern. Sustainable harvesting practices are encouraged to preserve the population of this mollusk, ensuring it remains a viable resource for future generations.
