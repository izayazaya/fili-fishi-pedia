Pitik-pitik

The pitik-pitik, also known as the "small shrimp" or "mantis shrimp," is a marine species found in the Philippines. It's a member of the stomatopod group, famous for its distinctive claws and vibrant coloration.

Common Name: Pitik-pitik

Scientific Name: Toxotes jaculatrix

Size: 10 cm - 20 cm

Weight: 0.1 kg - 0.5 kg

Season: Year-round peaking in May to October

Market Price(kg): PHP 120 - 200

Pitik-pitik can be found in shallow coastal waters, particularly in sandy and rocky areas, coral reefs, and mud flats along the Philippines' coastline.

The pitik-pitik is prized for its firm texture and unique flavor, making it a popular delicacy in local Filipino cuisine, especially in coastal regions. Its meat is often used in various dishes, including soups, stews, and stir-fries. Commercially, the fish is harvested using traditional fishing methods and is sold both fresh and frozen in local markets. Due to its relatively small size and strong claws, it requires skilled handling, often creating a niche market for those who specialize in harvesting and preparing the fish. Pitik-pitik is not only a culinary favorite but also a part of the local economy, supporting coastal communities with a sustainable source of income.
