Talimusak

The Talimusak fish, found in the Philippines, is scientifically known as Trichiurus lepturus, commonly referred to as the Largehead Hairtail.

Common Name: Talimusak

Scientific Name: Trichiurus lepturus

Size: 1 m - 2 m

Weight: 1 kg - 3 kg

Season: Year-round peaking in June - October

Market Price(kg): PHP 150 - 250

The Talimusak fish is found in the coastal waters of the Philippines, particularly in regions such as the Visayas and Mindanao. It is commonly found in deeper waters, though it also moves closer to the shore during breeding seasons.

Commercially, the Talimusak fish is valued for its long, slender body and tender, white flesh. It is commonly used in Filipino cuisine for dishes like sinigang (sour soup), adobo, and grilled preparations. Talimusak is also often dried or salted for preservation, making it a popular option in coastal areas where it is caught.
