Sagisi

The Sagisi fish, found in the Philippines, is scientifically known as Nemipterus virgatus, commonly referred to as the Striped Threadfin Bream.

Common Name: Sagisi

Scientific Name: Nemipterus virgatus

Size: 20 cm - 30 cm

Weight: 0.3 kg - 0.5 kg

Season: Year-round peaking in November - April

Market Price(kg): PHP 150 - 250

The Sagisi fish is found in the coastal waters of the Philippines, especially in shallow, sandy or muddy areas near coral reefs and estuaries. It is commonly found in regions like the Visayas, Mindanao, and Luzon, where it thrives in warm tropical waters.

Commercially, the Sagisi fish is popular in local markets due to its mild, sweet flavor and tender meat. It is often used in Filipino dishes such as sinigang (sour soup), paksiw (fish cooked in vinegar), or grilled preparations. The fish is commonly sold fresh and is highly valued for its affordability and versatility in cooking. While not as widely recognized as some larger fish species, Sagisi plays an important role in supporting small-scale and local fisheries, especially in coastal areas. The fish’s demand in the local seafood trade contributes to the livelihood of many fishermen, and it is also exported to international markets. Due to its mild taste and availability, Sagisi remains a staple fish in Filipino homes and a key part of the country’s seafood industry.
