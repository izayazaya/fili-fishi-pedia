Guso

Guso is a type of edible marine algae found in the coastal waters of the Philippines. Known for its unique texture and nutritional value, it is often used in local dishes, such as salads and soups, or as a garnish in various Filipino recipes.

Common Name: Guso

Scientific Name: Eucheuma denticulatum

Size: 30 cm - 60 cm

Weight: 0.2 kg - 1 kg

Season: Year-round peaking in March to July

Market Price(kg): PHP 50 - 100

Guso can be found along the coastal regions of the Philippines, especially in the provinces of Sorsogon, Davao, and Zamboanga. It thrives in shallow, warm waters, particularly in areas where there are coral reefs.

Commercially, Guso is considered a valuable marine resource, particularly in the Philippines, where it is harvested and sold as a culinary delicacy. It is often cultivated in sea farms to meet growing demand both locally and internationally. The algae is sought after for its mild, slightly salty flavor and is used in a variety of dishes. Additionally, it is valued for its nutritional content, being rich in fiber, minerals, and vitamins, making it a healthy ingredient in Filipino cuisine. Guso is also exported in dried or processed forms for international markets. With its growing popularity, the industry surrounding guso farming and harvesting has seen an increase, contributing to the livelihoods of coastal communities.
