Labahita

The Labahita fish, commonly found in the Philippines, is scientifically known as Seriola rivoliana, also called the Greater Yellowtail or Jackfish.

Common Name: Labahita

Scientific Name: Seriola rivoliana

Size: 1.5 m - 1.8 m

Weight: 5 kg - 15 kg

Season: Year-round peaking in November - April

Market Price(kg): PHP 250 - 400

Labahita is found in coastal waters across the Philippines, particularly in the Visayan and Mindanao regions. It is often spotted around reefs, drop-offs, and deeper waters where it preys on smaller fish and squid.

Commercially, the Labahita fish is highly regarded for its firm texture and mild, flavorful flesh. It is commonly used in Filipino cuisine for grilling, frying, or making sinigang (sour soup) and kinilaw (raw fish salad). Its popularity in local markets is significant, especially in coastal areas where fresh fish is a staple. Labahita is also in demand in export markets, contributing to the Philippine fishing industry, particularly for higher-end seafood exports. Its size and quality make it a favorite among fishers, and it supports both small-scale and commercial fisheries in the country.
