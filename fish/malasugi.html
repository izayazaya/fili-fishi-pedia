Malasugi

The Malasugi fish, found in the Philippines, is scientifically known as Coryphaena hippurus, commonly referred to as the Mahi-mahi or Dorado.

Common Name: Malasugi

Scientific Name: Coryphaena hippurus

Size: 1 m - 1.5 m

Weight: 8 kgs - 15 kgs

Season: Year-round peaking in November - April

Market Price(kg): PHP 300 - 600

The Malasugi fish is found in the open waters of the Philippines, particularly in coastal areas such as the Visayas, Mindanao, and Luzon. It is commonly found in deep waters near offshore reefs, drop-offs, and islands.

Commercially, the Malasugi fish is highly valued for its vibrant colors, firm texture, and rich flavor. It is popular in local markets and restaurants and is often used in grilling, frying, or making dishes like sinigang (sour soup) and kinilaw (raw fish salad). Known for its mild, sweet taste, the fish is especially sought after in both local and international markets. Malasugi is commonly sold fresh or frozen and is one of the more commercially significant fish species in the Philippine fishing industry. It is also important for the export market, with the fish being shipped to other countries due to its popularity as a sought-after food source.
