Paru-parong-dagat

Common Name: Paru-parong-dagat

Scientific Name: Chaetodon spp.

Size: 10 cm - 20 cm

Weight: 0.05 kg - 0.2 kg

Season: Year-round peaking in March - May

Market Price(kg): PHP 400 - 700

Paru-parong Dagat is widely distributed across coral reefs in the Philippines, particularly in regions with rich marine biodiversity, such as Tubbataha Reef, Palawan, Batangas, and the Visayas. They inhabit shallow reef zones, thriving in clear, warm waters.

Paru-parong Dagat, or butterflyfish, is renowned for its striking colors and patterns, making it a popular choice in the ornamental fish trade. While not a significant food source due to its small size, it contributes to local economies as an export product for aquariums worldwide. The collection of butterflyfish requires sustainable practices to protect coral reef ecosystems, as these fish are vital to reef health and biodiversity. Butterflyfish hold cultural and ecological importance in the Philippines, drawing divers and tourists, further boosting local livelihoods through eco-tourism.
