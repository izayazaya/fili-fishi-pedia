Bulate

The Snake Mackerel (Bulate) is a predatory fish known for its elongated, snake-like body and sharp teeth. This fish is known for its strong swimming abilities and is often caught in both commercial and small-scale fishing operations.

Common Name: Bulate

Scientific Name: Gempylus serpens

Size: 100 cm - 120 cm

Weight: 5 kg - 7 kgs

Season: Year-round peaking in March to August

Market Price(kg): PHP 100 - 200

The Snake Mackerel is found in deep, warm waters around the Philippines, particularly in the Visayan Sea, the Sulu Sea, and other coastal waters where they migrate for feeding and spawning. They are typically captured at depths ranging from 200 to 500 meters.

The Snake Mackerel, also known locally as Bulate, is not as commercially prominent as other fish species in the Philippines, but it still holds some importance in local fisheries. The fish is valued for its firm, white flesh, which is commonly processed into fillets or used in various Filipino dishes, often grilled or dried. Due to its relatively high fat content, the fish tends to have a strong, sometimes pungent flavor, which some people find less desirable. However, it is often preferred for its high nutritional value, especially its omega-3 fatty acids. It is sometimes exported or sold in markets catering to specific culinary tastes.
