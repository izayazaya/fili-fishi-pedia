Arosep

The Arosep is a fish commonly found in the Philippines, known for its significant role in local fishing industries.

Common Name: Arosep

Scientific Name: Gerrhosaurus hainanus

Size: 30 cm - 40 cm

Weight: 1 kg - 2 kg

Season: Year-round peaking in March to August

Market Price(kg): PHP 300 - 800

Arosep can be found in the shallow, coastal waters of the Philippines, especially in areas with coral reefs and mangrove ecosystems. They thrive in regions with warm water and are often located near fishing villages.

Commercially, Arosep is a notable catch for local fishermen, valued for its taste and nutritional content. It is a popular fish in Filipino cuisine, often served fresh or dried. Due to its availability in coastal areas, it plays an important role in supporting local livelihoods and is typically sold in markets for a modest price. Its flesh is tender, making it a preferred choice for a variety of dishes, from grilled fish to stews. Furthermore, its commercial availability supports small-scale fisheries and the broader seafood industry in the Philippines, contributing to food security and local economies.
