Gulyasan

Common Name: Gulyasan

Scientific Name: Acanthopagrus bifasciatus

Size: 25 cm - 30 cm

Weight: 0.2 kg - 0.5 kg

Season: Year-round peaking in November - April

Market Price(kg): PHP 400 - 450

Gulyusan is found in coastal waters and shallow reefs around the Philippines, particularly in areas like the Visayan Sea, Palawan, and coastal regions of Luzon and Mindanao. It prefers rocky or sandy bottoms and can often be found in estuarine areas and near coral reefs.

Gulyusan (Acanthopagrus bifasciatus) is a popular fish in the Philippines, known for its firm, white flesh and mild, sweet flavor. It is commonly caught by local fishermen using nets or handlines and is usually sold fresh in markets, though it can also be preserved through salting or drying. Gulyusan is valued for its versatility in cooking, making it suitable for grilling, frying, or steaming. It is often used in traditional Filipino dishes like sinigang (sour soup) or grilled fish. The fish is popular in local markets due to its relatively low cost and availability, and it supports small-scale fishing communities throughout the country. Efforts to ensure sustainable fishing practices help maintain the abundance of Gulyusan, which is important for both domestic consumption and the local fishing economy.
