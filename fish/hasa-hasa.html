Hasa-hasa

Common Name: Hasa-hasa

Scientific Name: Johnius macrorhynus

Size: 30 cm - 50 cm

Weight: 0.3 kg - 1 kg

Season: Year-round peaking in March - May

Market Price(kg): PHP 200 - 300

Hasa-hasa is commonly found in the shallow coastal waters of the Philippines, including estuaries, muddy bottoms, and coral reefs. It is abundant in regions like the Visayan Sea, Sulu Sea, and near the coasts of Luzon, Mindanao, and Palawan.

Hasa-hasa (Johnius spp.) is a commercially important fish in the Philippines, recognized for its tender, white flesh and mildly sweet flavor. It is widely caught using nets or traps and is typically sold fresh in local markets. Hasa-hasa is highly valued for its versatility in cooking, often grilled, fried, or used in stews and soups like sinigang. The fish is affordable and easy to prepare, making it a popular choice for home cooks and restaurants alike. Its availability year-round, combined with its relatively low cost, makes it a staple in Filipino households, particularly in coastal communities. Additionally, Hasa-hasa supports local fisheries, contributing to the livelihoods of many small-scale fishermen.
