Talaba

The talaba, or oyster, is a marine mollusk commonly found in coastal waters. Known for its bivalve shell, it has a unique flavor and texture, making it a popular seafood item in the Philippines.

Common Name: Talaba

Scientific Name: Crassostrea iredalei

Size: 7 cm - 10 cm

Weight: 0.1 kgs - 0.3 kgs

Season: Year-round peaking in March to September

Market Price(kg): PHP 100 - 200

Talaba can be found in various coastal areas across the Philippines, particularly in regions such as Batangas, Cavite, Zambales, and Pangasinan. They thrive in brackish waters, often near estuaries and shallow bays.

Commercially, talaba is harvested for its edible meat and highly valued for its rich, briny taste. It plays a significant role in the Philippines' aquaculture industry, with large-scale farming operations dedicated to oyster cultivation. The harvesting process involves collecting oysters from beds and carefully opening the shells to extract the meat. The market for talaba is robust, especially in local seafood restaurants and markets where fresh oysters are served raw, grilled, or cooked in various dishes. Due to their delicious taste and high nutritional value, talaba continues to be a staple in Filipino cuisine and an essential part of the coastal economy.
