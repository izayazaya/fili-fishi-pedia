Kalaso

The Kalaso fish, commonly found in the Philippines, is scientifically known as Scomberomorus commerson, also called the Spanish Mackerel.

Common Name: Kalaso

Scientific Name: Scomberomorus commerson

Size: 1.2 m - 1.5 m

Weight: 5 kg - 7 kg

Season: Year-round peaking in November - May

Market Price(kg): PHP 150 - 250

Kalaso is found throughout the Philippines, particularly in coastal waters, and is commonly located in areas with rich marine ecosystems like the Visayan Sea and the Sulu Sea. It prefers deeper waters and is often found near reefs, drop-offs, or in open ocean areas.

Commercially, Kalaso is highly prized for its rich, flavorful, and oily meat, which makes it popular in both local and international markets. It is often sold fresh, frozen, or smoked and is commonly used in Filipino dishes such as grilled mackerel, kinilaw (raw fish salad), and tinola (a savory soup). Its firm texture and high oil content make it a favorite for grilling or smoking, which enhances its flavor. Kalaso is an important fish in the Philippine fishing industry, especially for export to neighboring countries, contributing to both local consumption and the country's seafood trade.
