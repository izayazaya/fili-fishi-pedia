Tawilis

The Tawilis fish, found in the Philippines, is scientifically known as Hypseleotris agilis, commonly referred to as the Philippine Freshwater Sardine.

Common Name: Tawilis

Scientific Name: Hypseleotris agilis

Size: 15 cm - 20 cm

Weight: 0.02 kg - 0.05 kg

Season: Year-round peaking in November - May

Market Price(kg): PHP 200 - 350

The Tawilis fish is endemic to the Philippines and can only be found in Taal Lake in Batangas, Luzon, making it a unique species in the country. This small, silvery fish thrives in freshwater environments, especially in the cool, clean waters of Taal Lake.

Commercially, the Tawilis fish is prized for its mild flavor, soft texture, and high nutritional value. It is often consumed fresh, fried, or smoked, and is used in a variety of Filipino dishes, including sinigang (sour soup), sapsap, and kilawin (raw fish marinated in vinegar). Due to its limited habitat, the Tawilis is considered a delicacy, and it is primarily sold in local markets in Batangas and nearby regions. However, overfishing and habitat degradation have led to concerns about the species' sustainability. As a result, conservation efforts have been made to regulate its catch to ensure the long-term survival of the Tawilis. Despite being locally available, its scarcity and specific habitat make it a prized fish in Filipino culinary culture.
