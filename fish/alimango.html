Alimango

"Alimango," commonly known as the mud crab, is a highly valued aquatic species in the Philippines. Renowned for its sweet and delicate flavor, this crustacean is often harvested for local and international markets. It thrives in brackish and mangrove-rich environments, playing a vital role in coastal ecosystems and the aquaculture industry.

Common Name: Alimango

Scientific Name: Scylla serrata

Size: 15 cm - 20 cm

Weight: 0.5 kg - 2 kg

Season: November to May

Market Price(kg): PHP 700 - 1200

Alimango is commonly found in mangroves, estuaries, and brackish water environments along coastal regions of the Philippines. Provinces like Palawan, Samar, Bohol, and Pangasinan are known for their abundant mud crab populations.

Alimango, or mud crab, is one of the most economically significant crustaceans in the Philippines. Valued for its flavorful meat and robust market demand, it is harvested both from the wild and through aquaculture systems. Alimango farming, often done in brackish water ponds and mangrove areas, contributes substantially to the livelihood of many Filipino fisherfolk. The crabs are exported live or processed, especially to Asian markets where they are considered a delicacy. High-value species like Scylla serrata are prized for their size, weight, and culinary versatility, making them a centerpiece of local and global seafood industries.
