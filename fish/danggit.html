Danggit

Common Name: Danggit

Scientific Name: Siganus spp.

Size: 15 cm - 30 cm

Weight: 0.2 kg - 0.5 kg

Season: Year-round peaking in November - May

Market Price(kg): PHP 450 - 730

Danggit is widely distributed in the Philippines and is commonly found in shallow coastal waters, seagrass beds, and coral reefs. Regions such as Cebu, Palawan, and Mindanao are notable for their rabbitfish populations. Danggit prefers areas with abundant vegetation and can be seen grazing on algae.

Danggit is a highly valued fish in the Philippines, particularly as a dried fish delicacy popular in Cebu and other regions. Its tender flesh and mild flavor make it suitable for drying, grilling, or frying, contributing to its demand in both local and export markets. The fish is often caught using nets or fish traps in shallow waters, and its adaptability to aquaculture has further boosted its availability. Danggit farming, especially for fry production, has gained traction as an economic activity, supporting livelihoods in coastal communities. Its role as a staple food and as a delicacy underscores its significance in Filipino cuisine and culture.
