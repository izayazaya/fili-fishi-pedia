Pugita

Pugita is a common name used for octopus species found in the Philippines, particularly the Octopus cyanea. It is known for its unique, soft-bodied, and highly intelligent nature.

Common Name: Pugita

Scientific Name: Octopus cyanea

Size: Up to 60 cm

Weight: 3 kg - 5 kg

Season: Year-round peaking in June to September

Market Price(kg): PHP 300 - 600

Pugita, or the day octopus, can be found in coastal areas, particularly in the shallow waters of the Visayan Sea, Palawan, and Mindanao. It thrives in coral reefs and sandy bottoms, often hiding in crevices.

The Pugita is an important species in Philippine fisheries, both for local consumption and export. Its meat is prized for its tenderness, versatility, and distinct flavor. In commercial fishing, pugita is often caught using traps or by hand, and it is commonly sold fresh or frozen. It plays a significant role in the seafood industry, especially in areas with high demand for octopus in local delicacies like adobong pugita or in international markets. Despite being widely available, the species' popularity has led to concerns about overfishing, urging sustainable practices in its harvest to ensure long-term availability.
