Chabeta

Common Name: Chabeta

Scientific Name: Mene maculata

Size: 25 cm - 30 cm

Weight: 0.5 kg - 1 kg

Season: Year-round

Market Price(kg): PHP 70 - 150

The moonfish, locally known as "chabeta," is commonly found in coastal and offshore waters throughout the Philippines. They inhabit areas near coral reefs, rocky substrates, and open waters, where they feed on small crustaceans and plankton.

The moonfish (Mene maculata) is a commercially significant fish in the Philippines due to its tender, white flesh and delicate flavor. It is a popular choice for various Filipino dishes, such as "pinangat" and fried fish recipes, as it adapts well to diverse cooking methods. Moonfish are often caught by small-scale fishermen using nets, making them accessible in local wet markets. Their year-round availability and affordability contribute to their popularity as a staple protein source for Filipino households and restaurants alike.
