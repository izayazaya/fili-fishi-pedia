Galunggong

Common Name: Galunggong

Scientific Name: Decapterus maruadsi

Size: 30 cm - 35 cm

Weight: 0.1 kg - 0.2 kg

Season: Year-round peaking in December - April

Market Price(kg): PHP 240 - 360

Galungong is commonly found in coastal waters, near islands, reefs, and continental shelves in the Philippines. It is distributed throughout the country, including regions like the Visayan Sea, Sulu Sea, and coastal areas of Luzon, Mindanao, and Palawan. The species prefers shallow, nearshore waters where it forms large schools.

Galungong (Decapterus maruadsi) is one of the most commercially important fish in the Philippines due to its abundance, affordability, and versatility in cooking. It is commonly sold fresh, dried, or preserved in various forms, such as salted or smoked, making it a staple in many Filipino households. Galungong is used in a wide range of dishes, from fried and grilled preparations to stews and soups. Its mild flavor and firm texture make it a favorite for various cooking styles. The fish is often caught by small-scale fishermen using nets, and large commercial fisheries also target it for both local consumption and export. Galungong plays a significant role in the local fishing economy and is popular in both traditional and modern Filipino cuisine.
