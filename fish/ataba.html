Ataba

Common Name: Ataba

Scientific Name: Rastrelliger brachysoma

Size: 20 cm - 25 cm

Weight: 0.15 kg - 0.25 kg

Season: Year-round peaking in March - July

Market Price(kg): PHP 150 - 570

The Ataba fish is commonly found in coastal and offshore waters of the Philippines, particularly in the Visayas and Mindanao regions. Major fishing grounds include the Visayan Sea, Moro Gulf, and the waters surrounding Palawan and Samar.

The Ataba, a type of short-bodied mackerel, is a significant resource for the Philippine fishing industry due to its abundance and economic value. Known for its delicate flavor and versatility, Ataba is a staple in local markets and is widely consumed in various forms, such as fresh, dried, or canned. It thrives in warm, tropical waters, often forming large schools that make it relatively easy to harvest. The fish is in high demand for both local consumption and export, contributing to the livelihood of many Filipino fishing communities.
