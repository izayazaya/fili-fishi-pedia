Sugpo

Sugpo (Penaeus monodon), commonly known as the black tiger shrimp, is a species of large marine shrimp found in the coastal waters of the Philippines.

Common Name: Sugpo

Scientific Name: Penaeus monodon

Size: Up to 33 cm

Weight: 0.2 kg - 0.4 kg

Season: Year-round peaking in March to June

Market Price(kg): PHP 500 - 1200

Sugpo can be found in the brackish waters of coastal areas, particularly in the provinces of Pangasinan, Zambales, and Bataan. It thrives in estuaries and is commonly farmed in aquaculture ponds throughout the country.

Sugpo is one of the most valuable seafood exports of the Philippines, highly sought after in both domestic and international markets. Its large size, firm texture, and sweet flavor make it a favorite in high-end restaurants, where it is often served in dishes like grilled shrimp, shrimp cocktails, and shrimp tempura. The Philippines is one of the largest producers of black tiger shrimp, and the farming industry is a significant contributor to the local economy. However, the industry also faces challenges related to sustainability, disease management, and environmental impact, prompting efforts to develop more eco-friendly aquaculture practices.
