Alumahang-bato

Common Name: Alumahang-bato

Scientific Name: Rastrelliger spp.

Size: 20 cm - 25 cm

Weight: 0.2 kg - 0.5 kg

Season: Year-round with peaking in March - June

Market Price(kg): PHP 220 - 320

The Alumahang-Bato, or stone mackerel, is commonly found in coastal and marine waters around the Philippines. It thrives in areas such as bays, estuaries, and the continental shelf, particularly in regions rich in plankton and other small prey. Popular fishing spots include waters around Palawan, Visayas, and the northern and southern Luzon coasts.

The Alumahang-Bato, a member of the mackerel family, holds economic importance in the Philippines due to its accessibility and popularity as a food source. Its firm, flavorful flesh makes it a staple in various Filipino dishes, often grilled, fried, or made into soups. It is primarily caught using purse seines, gill nets, or traditional fishing methods, contributing to local livelihoods. The fish is widely available in wet markets and grocery stores, often sold fresh, frozen, or dried.
