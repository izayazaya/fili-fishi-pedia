Maming

Common Name: Maming

Scientific Name: Scarus spp.

Size: 20 cm - 50 cm

Weight: 0.5 kg - 2 kg

Season: Year-round peaking in March - May

Market Price(kg): PHP 150 - 250

Maming is found in coral reefs and lagoons across the Philippines, particularly in marine-rich areas like Palawan, Cebu, and the Visayas. It thrives in clear, shallow waters with vibrant reef ecosystems.

Maming, or parrotfish, is a sought-after species in the Philippines due to its colorful appearance and tasty meat. It is commonly sold in markets as fresh or dried fish and is a popular choice for grilling, steaming, or as an ingredient in traditional Filipino soups and stews. Despite its popularity, overfishing poses a threat to its population as parrotfish play a crucial ecological role in maintaining reef health. Commercially, Maming contributes to the income of local fishermen, particularly in coastal regions, and is an integral part of subsistence fishing. Sustainable practices are encouraged to balance its economic value with environmental conservation.
