Alumahan

The Alumahan is a commercially important fish species in the Philippines, widely recognized for its taste and culinary versatility.

Common Name: Alumahan

Scientific Name: Selar crumenophthalmus

Size: 25 cm - 35 cm

Weight: 0.4 kg - 1 kg

Season: Year-round with peaking in March - June

Market Price(kg): PHP 280 - 400

Alumahan is commonly found in the warm, coastal waters of the Philippines, particularly in areas near reefs, bays, and offshore islands. It is abundant in both shallow and deeper waters, often in schools near the surface.

The Alumahan is highly prized in the Philippine fishing industry for its flavor and versatility in cooking. It is often sold fresh, dried, or salted and is a staple in many Filipino households. The fish is favored for dishes like sinigang (a sour soup), grilled, or used in stews. It plays a significant role in the local economy, supporting both small-scale fisheries and the larger seafood trade. Its abundant presence in the Philippines ensures a steady supply, making it a common choice for domestic markets and exports. Additionally, its relatively low price and high demand make it an accessible source of protein for many Filipinos.
