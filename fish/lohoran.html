Lohoran

The Lohoran fish, commonly found in the Philippines, is scientifically known as Siganus guttatus, also referred to as the Rabbitfish.

Common Name: Lohoran

Scientific Name: Siganus guttatus

Size: 35 cm - 45 cm

Weight: 0.5 kg - 1 kg

Season: Year-round peaking in November - April

Market Price(kg): PHP 200 - 400

The Lohoran fish is found in shallow coastal waters, typically around coral reefs, seagrass beds, and lagoons. It is abundant in the Philippines, especially in regions like the Visayas, Mindanao, and Luzon, where it thrives in warm, tropical waters.

Commercially, the Lohoran fish is highly valued for its tender meat and mild flavor. It is commonly sold fresh in local markets and is used in a variety of Filipino dishes such as sinigang (sour soup), grilled preparations, and kinilaw (raw fish salad). Lohoran is also popular in coastal areas, where it is often farmed due to its adaptability and fast growth. Its demand is steadily increasing, both locally and for export, making it a significant part of the Philippine seafood industry. The Lohoran fish supports the livelihoods of many small-scale fishermen and fish farmers, especially in rural and coastal communities.
