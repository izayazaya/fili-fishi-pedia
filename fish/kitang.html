Kitang

The Kitang fish, commonly found in the Philippines, is scientifically known as Leptobarbus hoevenii, and is often referred to as the Indonesian carp or Kitang in local terms.

Common Name: Kitang

Scientific Name: Leptobarbus hoevenii

Size: 35 cm - 50 cm

Weight: 0.5 kg - 1 kg

Season: Year-round peaking in June - November

Market Price(kg): PHP 500 - 600

Kitang is commonly found in the Philippines, particularly in the rivers, lakes, and freshwater systems of Luzon, Visayas, and Mindanao. It prefers slow-moving or still waters like ponds, marshes, and lowland rivers.

Commercially, the Kitang fish is appreciated for its mild taste and tender flesh, making it popular in local Filipino dishes such as tinola (fish soup) and paksiw (fish cooked in vinegar). This fish is often sold fresh in local markets and is an affordable source of protein in many households, particularly in rural areas. While it is not as commercially widespread as some other species, it holds a place in traditional Filipino cuisine and small-scale fish farming. Kitang contributes to local fisheries and sustains the livelihoods of people in inland communities.
