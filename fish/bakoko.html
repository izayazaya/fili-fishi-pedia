Bakoko

The Bakoko is a small, commercially important fish found in the Philippines, especially valued for its use in traditional Filipino dishes.

Common Name: Bakoko

Scientific Name: Stolephorus baculoides

Size: 8 cm - 12 cm

Weight: 0.01 kg - 0.02 kg

Season: Year-round peaking in June - September

Market Price(kg): PHP 220 - 500

The Bakoko is found in the coastal waters of the Philippines, particularly in shallow areas such as estuaries, bays, and near mangroves. It thrives in warm, clear waters and is distributed across many regions of the country, including areas in the Visayan and Mindanao seas.

The Bakoko is commercially significant in the Philippines due to its role in producing fish products like bagoong (fermented fish paste) and alamang (fermented shrimp paste). It is often caught in large quantities by local fishermen and is either sold fresh or preserved through drying and salting. This small fish is a staple in many Filipino households, used in various dishes such as stews, soups, and as a condiment for rice. The Bakoko supports local fisheries, especially in rural coastal areas, and provides a source of income for many fishermen. Its abundance and culinary versatility make it an essential part of both the local diet and the broader seafood trade in the Philippines.
