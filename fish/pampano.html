Pampano

The Pampano fish, found in the Philippines, is scientifically known as Trachinotus blochii, commonly referred to as the Golden Pomfret.

Common Name: Pampano

Scientific Name: Trachinotus blochii

Size: 45 cm - 60 cm

Weight: 1 kg - 2 kgs

Season: Year-round peaking in November - April

Market Price(kg): PHP 100 - 200

The Pampano fish is found in the coastal waters of the Philippines, particularly in shallow, sandy or muddy bottoms near coral reefs and estuaries. It is commonly found in the Visayas, Mindanao, and parts of Luzon.

Commercially, the Pampano fish is highly valued for its delicate, sweet flavor and tender, white meat. It is one of the most popular fish in the Philippines, commonly used in a variety of Filipino dishes such as sinigang (sour soup), paksiw (fish cooked in vinegar), or simply grilled. Pampano is available fresh in local markets and is also farmed in certain coastal regions. Due to its fast growth rate and relatively low maintenance, it has become a significant species in aquaculture, contributing to both local and commercial fisheries. Its mild flavor and affordability make it a staple in Filipino households, while its market demand also drives export trade. The Pampano plays a crucial role in the seafood industry, supporting the livelihoods of many fishermen and fish farmers in the Philippines.
