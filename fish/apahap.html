Apahap

The Apahap is a popular fish in the Philippines, valued for its size and flavor, commonly found in local waters.

Common Name: Apahap

Scientific Name: Lutjanus bohar

Size: 50 cm - 60 cm

Weight: 1 kg - 4 kg

Season: Year-round peaking in March - June

Market Price(kg): PHP 820 - 920

The Apahap is found in the warm, coastal waters of the Philippines, especially near coral reefs, rocky areas, and in deeper offshore waters. It is abundant in both the Visayan Sea and other coastal regions throughout the Philippines.

The Apahap is commercially valuable and widely sought after in the Philippines for its firm, white meat and mild flavor. It is commonly sold fresh in local markets, often grilled, fried, or used in dishes like sinigang (a sour soup) and kinilaw (Filipino ceviche). Due to its excellent eating quality, it is a favorite in Filipino households and restaurants. The fish is also exported, contributing to the Philippine seafood industry. The Apahap supports both small-scale and large-scale fisheries, providing a livelihood for many coastal communities. Its availability and versatility make it a key species in Filipino culinary traditions and the commercial fishing sector.
