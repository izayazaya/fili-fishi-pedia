Ayungin

The Ayungin is a small but significant fish in the Philippines, highly valued for its taste and its role in local fisheries.

Common Name: Ayungin

Scientific Name: Leiognathus equulus

Size: 15 cm - 20 cm

Weight: 0.05 kg - 0.1 kg

Season: Year-round peaking in March - June

Market Price(kg): PHP 300 - 500

The Ayungin is commonly found in the shallow coastal waters of the Philippines, particularly in areas with sandy or muddy bottoms, often near mangroves and estuaries. It is widespread throughout the Philippine archipelago, especially in the Visayan and Mindanao regions.

Ayungin is a commercially important fish in the Philippines due to its popularity in local markets and its use in a variety of traditional dishes. It is often caught in large numbers, either fresh or dried, and is a key ingredient in Filipino cuisine, particularly in dishes like sinigang (a sour soup) and kinilaw (a raw fish salad). Its mild flavor and tender flesh make it a favorite among Filipinos. Additionally, Ayungin is often preserved by salting or drying for storage and export. The fish provides a source of livelihood for many small-scale fishermen, especially in rural coastal communities, and contributes significantly to the local economy and food security. Its abundant supply and versatile use in cooking make it a staple in Filipino households.
