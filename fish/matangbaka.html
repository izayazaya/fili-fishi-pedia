Matangbaka

The Matangbaka fish, found in the Philippines, is scientifically known as Scomberomorus commerson, commonly referred to as the Spanish Mackerel.

Common Name: Matangbaka

Scientific Name: Scomberomorus commerson

Size: 1.2 m - 1.5 m

Weight: 5 kg - 15 kg

Season: Year-round peaking in November - April

Market Price(kg): PHP 250 - 400

Matangbaka is found in the coastal waters of the Philippines, particularly around the Visayas, Mindanao, and parts of Luzon. It is commonly spotted in deeper waters, often near reefs and offshore islands.

Commercially, the Matangbaka fish is highly valued for its firm, flavorful flesh and is popular for grilling, frying, and making dishes like sinigang (sour soup) or kinilaw (raw fish salad). It is widely available in local markets and is considered a premium fish due to its rich taste and versatility in various cooking methods. Matangbaka is also an important species for the export market, especially in the form of frozen fillets or whole fish. Its demand contributes significantly to the Philippine fishing industry, supporting both small-scale and commercial fisheries. With its high nutritional value and distinct taste, Matangbaka remains a popular choice for both local consumers and international seafood markets.
