Asohos

The Asohos is a small but commercially valuable fish found in the Philippines, known for its delicate flavor and use in local cuisine.

Common Name: Asohos

Scientific Name: Stolephorus devisi

Size: 10 cm - 12 cm

Weight: 10 g - 20 g

Season: Year-round peaking in March - June

Market Price(kg): PHP 700 - 750

Asohos is abundant in the coastal waters of the Philippines, especially in regions with abundant plankton and in shallow areas near estuaries, bays, and mangroves. It thrives in areas with clear, warm waters and is commonly found in the Visayan Sea, the Sulu Sea, and other coastal regions.

Commercially, Asohos is highly valued in the Philippines, particularly in the production of fish products like bagoong (fermented fish paste) and alamang (fermented shrimp paste). It is also consumed fresh or dried, often used as a seasoning in various Filipino dishes such as sinigang and adobo. Due to its small size and abundance, Asohos is typically caught in large quantities, making it a staple in both local markets and regional exports. The fish plays a key role in supporting small-scale fisheries and provides a source of livelihood for coastal communities. Its versatility and importance in Filipino cuisine make it a significant commercial species, especially in the southern parts of the country.
