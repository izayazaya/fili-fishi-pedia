Kanduli

The Kanduli fish, found in the Philippines, is scientifically known as Arius manillensis, and is a type of catfish, commonly referred to as the Manila Catfish.

Common Name: Kanduli

Scientific Name: Arius manillensis

Size: 35 cm - 50 cm

Weight: 1 kg - 2 kgs

Season: Year-round peaking in June - October

Market Price(kg): PHP 100 - 200

Kanduli is found in freshwater and brackish waters across the Philippines, particularly in rivers, lakes, and coastal estuaries. It is abundant in areas such as Luzon, Visayas, and Mindanao, where it thrives in mud or sandy riverbeds.

Commercially, the Kanduli fish is highly valued in Filipino cuisine due to its firm texture and distinct flavor. It is often used in traditional dishes such as sinigang na kanduli (sour fish soup) and grilled or fried preparations. The fish is sold fresh, smoked, or preserved in salt, making it a staple in local markets and an important part of the Philippine seafood trade. Kanduli is especially popular among coastal communities where it is an affordable and readily available source of protein, contributing significantly to the livelihoods of local fishermen.
