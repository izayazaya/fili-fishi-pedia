Lao-lao

The Lao-lao fish, found in the Philippines, is scientifically known as Saurida tumbil, commonly referred to as the Greater Lizardfish.

Common Name: Lao-lao

Scientific Name: Saurida tumbil

Size: 75 cm - 90 cm

Weight: 1 kg - 3 kg

Season: Year-round peaking in June - September

Market Price(kg): PHP 150 - 300

The Lao-lao fish can be found in coastal waters, especially in sandy or muddy bottoms near reefs and estuaries across the Philippines, particularly in the Visayas, Mindanao, and Luzon regions.

Commercially, the Lao-lao fish is known for its firm, white flesh and mild flavor, making it a popular choice for grilling, frying, and making soups. It is commonly found in local markets, where it is sold fresh or dried. While not as commercially prominent as some other fish species, Lao-lao is still an important catch for small-scale fishermen and coastal communities. It is often used in traditional Filipino dishes like sinigang (sour soup) and escabeche (sweet and sour fish). Lao-lao contributes to the local seafood trade and provides a reliable source of protein for many coastal residents.
