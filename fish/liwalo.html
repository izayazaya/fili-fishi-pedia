Liwalo

The Liwalo fish, found in the Philippines, is scientifically known as Nemipterus spp., commonly referred to as Threadfin Bream.

Common Name: Liwalo

Scientific Name: Nemipterus spp.

Size: 20 cm - 30 cm

Weight: 0.2 kg - 0.5 kg

Season: Year-round peaking in November - April

Market Price(kg): PHP 180 - 350

The Liwalo fish is found in the coastal waters of the Philippines, especially in sandy or muddy bottoms near coral reefs, estuaries, and shallow areas. It is commonly found in the Visayas, Mindanao, and parts of Luzon.

Commercially, the Liwalo fish is highly regarded for its tender meat and mild, sweet flavor. It is commonly sold fresh in local markets and is often used in Filipino dishes such as sinigang (sour soup), grilled fish, or paksiw (fish cooked in vinegar). Liwalo is also popular in coastal areas, where it is a common catch for small-scale fishermen. Its mild taste and affordability make it a popular choice for everyday meals. Although not as commercially significant as some larger fish species, Liwalo still plays a crucial role in the local seafood industry, supporting the livelihoods of many coastal communities. The fish is also in demand for export in certain markets, adding to its commercial value.
