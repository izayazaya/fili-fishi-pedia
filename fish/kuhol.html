Kuhol

The Kuhol is a freshwater mollusk, also known as the "apple snail." It is commonly found in the Philippines' freshwater ecosystems, particularly in ponds, rice fields, and slow-moving bodies of water.

Common Name: Kuhol

Scientific Name: Pomacea canaliculata

Size: 8 cm - 10 cm

Weight: Around 0.05 kg

Season: Year-round peaking in June to November

Market Price(kg): PHP 150 - 250

The kuhol is widely distributed in various parts of the Philippines, particularly in freshwater habitats such as rice fields, ponds, and marshes. It thrives in areas with slow-moving water, often in provinces with extensive agricultural activities.

The kuhol, or apple snail, is a popular delicacy in Filipino cuisine, particularly in provinces like Pampanga and Cavite. It is harvested from rice paddies and freshwater habitats, where it is often found in large numbers. The snail is known for its firm texture and mild, slightly sweet flavor, making it ideal for various traditional dishes such as kohol sa gata (snails cooked in coconut milk). While its primary use is in local culinary traditions, the kuhol is also a subject of interest in ecological studies due to its impact on rice farming, where its feeding behavior can damage crops. Despite this, it remains an important source of food and livelihood in rural communities.
