Dalagang-bukid

Common Name: Dalagang-bukid

Scientific Name: Caesio cuning

Size: 20 cm - 30 cm

Weight: 0.2 kg - 0.4 kg

Season: Year-round peaking in November - May

Market Price(kg): PHP 300 - 400

Dalagang Bukid is commonly found in shallow coastal waters, coral reefs, and rocky areas throughout the Philippine archipelago, including the waters surrounding Luzon, Visayas, and Mindanao. It is a schooling fish, often spotted in large groups in reef ecosystems.

Dalagang Bukid (Caesio cuning) is a commercially valuable fish in the Philippines, known for its firm, white meat and mild flavor, which makes it a favorite in local cuisine. It is commonly prepared in traditional dishes such as "escabeche" (sweet and sour pickled fish) and fried fish recipes. Fisherfolk typically catch Dalagang Bukid using gill nets, handlines, or fish traps, often in nearshore fishing grounds. Its year-round availability ensures a consistent supply for wet markets and restaurants, contributing to its popularity among Filipino consumers. Sustainable fishing practices and aquaculture initiatives are being promoted to maintain healthy populations and support the livelihoods of local fishing communities.
