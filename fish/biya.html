Biya

The Biya is a small but commercially significant fish found in the Philippines, known for its availability in local fisheries and use in Filipino cuisine.

Common Name: Biya

Scientific Name: Glossogobius giuris

Size: 10 cm - 20 cm

Weight: 10 g - 30 g

Season: Year-round peaking in June - October

Market Price(kg): PHP 700 - 750

The Biya is commonly found in the freshwater and brackish water environments of the Philippines, particularly in rivers, lakes, and estuaries. It is commonly located in the Visayas, Mindanao, and other regions with rich aquatic habitats. The species is often found in slow-moving streams and the shallow areas of larger bodies of water.

The Biya is a popular fish in local markets and Filipino cuisine, known for its tender flesh and slightly sweet taste. It is typically used in dishes like sinigang (sour soup), pinangat (a type of fish stew), or grilled preparations. The fish is also a key ingredient in making bagoong (fermented fish paste), which is a widely used condiment in Filipino cooking. Its small size and abundance make it an accessible and affordable fish for both domestic consumption and commercial use. The Biya is also an important part of the local fishing industry, supporting livelihoods in rural and coastal communities across the Philippines.
