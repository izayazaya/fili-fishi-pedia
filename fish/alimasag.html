Alimasag

The Alimasag is a type of swimming crab commonly found in the Philippines. It is a widely recognized seafood delicacy, appreciated for its tender and flavorful meat.

Common Name: Alimasag

Scientific Name: Portunus pelagicus

Size: 10 cm - 20 cm

Weight: 0.2 kg - 0.5 kgs

Season: May to August.

Market Price(kg): PHP 600 - 700

Alimasag is abundant in coastal areas, particularly in estuaries, mangroves, sandy or muddy seabeds, and shallow coastal waters. Regions like Pangasinan, Bicol, and Palawan are known for their blue swimming crab populations.

The Alimasag is a commercially valuable species in the Philippines, driving both local fisheries and export markets. Its tender, sweet meat is sought after in the food industry, making it a staple in Filipino dishes like crab stews and curries. Blue swimming crabs are harvested from the wild and sometimes farmed, with their sustainability being a growing focus. The crabs are sold fresh, frozen, or processed into crabmeat, catering to both domestic demand and international export markets.
