Lapu-lapu

The Lapu-lapu fish, a prized catch in the Philippines, is scientifically known as Epinephelinae, a subfamily of groupers.

Common Name: Lapu-lapu

Scientific Name: Epinephelinae

Size: 1 m - 1.2 m

Weight: 5 kg - 15 kg

Season: Year-round peaking in November - May

Market Price(kg): PHP 500 - 1200

Lapu-lapu is commonly found in coastal waters, particularly around coral reefs, lagoons, and rocky outcrops throughout the Philippines. It thrives in tropical waters, especially in areas like the Visayan Sea, Sulu Sea, and surrounding waters of Luzon and Mindanao.

Commercially, Lapu-lapu is highly valued due to its firm, white, and flavorful flesh, making it a favorite in high-end restaurants and local markets. It is often served grilled, steamed, or made into sinigang (sour soup) or kinilaw (raw fish salad). Lapu-lapu is a significant fish in the Philippine seafood industry, contributing both to local consumption and export markets. Its high market demand has led to the establishment of fish farms in various regions to ensure a steady supply, making it an important species for both small-scale and large-scale fisheries. Lapu-lapu's commercial value and culinary reputation make it one of the Philippines' most sought-after fish species.
