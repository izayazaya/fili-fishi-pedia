Tuyom

Tuyom is a small species of fish commonly found in the coastal waters of the Philippines. It is typically recognized for its small size and is often used as a source of food for local communities.

Common Name: Tuyom

Scientific Name: Mulloidichthys martinicus

Size: 20 cm - 30 cm

Weight: 0.1 kg - 0.25 kg

Season: Year-round peaking in March to May

Market Price(kg): PHP 500 - 1500

Tuyom is found in shallow coastal waters, typically around coral reefs, sandy bottoms, and seagrass beds. It is widely distributed throughout the Philippines, particularly in regions such as Palawan, Cebu, and Bohol.

Commercially, Tuyom is valued for its local consumption and is often caught in large quantities using traditional fishing methods. It is usually sold fresh or dried and is a popular ingredient in Filipino cuisine, especially in soups, stews, and other seafood dishes. Though not as widely known internationally, it holds significance in the local markets, contributing to the fishing industry's economy in the coastal areas of the Philippines. Its small size makes it accessible for everyday meals, and it is favored for its delicate flavor.
