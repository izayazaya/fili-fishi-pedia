Dilat

In the Philippines, the term "dilat" commonly refers to the red bigeye fish, scientifically known as Priacanthus macracanthus. This species is recognized by its large eyes and vibrant red coloration.

Common Name: Dilat

Scientific Name: Priacanthus macracanthus

Size: 25 cm - 35 cm

Weight: 200 g - 500 g

Season: Year-round peaking in November - May

Market Price(kg): PHP 450 - 500

The red bigeye inhabits deeper waters, typically between 20 to 200 meters in depth. It is commonly found in marine, reef-associated environments throughout the Philippines, including regions near Luzon, Visayas, and Mindanao. Fishermen often target this species using shrimp, small fish, and crustaceans as bait.

Commercially, the red bigeye is valued for its firm, white flesh and mild flavor, making it a popular choice in Filipino cuisine. It is commonly prepared in various dishes, including stews and fried preparations. The fish is typically caught using handlines or traps, and its availability supports local fishing communities. Efforts to manage sustainable fishing practices are important to ensure the continued abundance of this valuable species.
