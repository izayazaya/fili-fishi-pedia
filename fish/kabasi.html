Kabasi

The Kabasi fish, commonly found in the Philippines, is scientifically known as Lutjanus argentimaculatus, also called the Golden Snapper or Mangrove Red Snapper. It is popular in local markets due to its appealing taste and texture.

Common Name: Kabasi

Scientific Name: Lutjanus argentimaculatus

Size: 40 cm - 60 cm

Weight: 2 kgs - 4 kgs

Season: Year-round peaking in November - May

Market Price(kg): PHP 300 - 400

Kabasi can be found in the Philippines, particularly in coastal areas, mangrove swamps, and near coral reefs. It is commonly found in regions such as the Visayas, Mindanao, and the surrounding islands.

Commercially, the Kabasi fish is highly sought after for its delicate, sweet flavor and firm texture. It is a preferred fish for both domestic consumption and export. It is commonly sold fresh in local markets and is used in various Filipino dishes like sinigang (sour soup), grilled preparations, and fish stews. Its high commercial demand makes it an important species in the local fishing industry, supporting both small-scale fishermen and larger seafood enterprises in the Philippines.
