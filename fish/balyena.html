Balyena

Common Name: Balyena

Scientific Name: Balaenoptera musculus

Size: Up to 30 m

Weight: 180000 kg

Season: December to May

Market Price(kg): PHP 250 - 650

Whales, or balyena, can be found in the waters around the Philippines, especially in areas such as Bohol, Mindanao, and Palawan. The country is a popular destination for whale watching, particularly for species like the humpback whale and the sperm whale. These whales are migratory and can be seen in Philippine waters as they pass through during migration.

Balyena, or whales, are not commercially harvested in the Philippines due to national and international protections. However, the Philippines is known for whale-watching tourism, where tourists flock to regions like Bohol and Palawan to observe these majestic creatures in their natural habitat. The country's government, along with conservation organizations, has enacted policies to protect whale populations and promote eco-tourism as a sustainable industry. Whale watching has become an important aspect of eco-tourism in the Philippines, providing jobs and supporting local economies without harming the whales. Whales are seen as a symbol of biodiversity conservation, and their presence has helped raise awareness about the need to protect marine ecosystems.
