Talangka

Talangka, also known as the mud crab, is a small but highly prized species found in the coastal waters of the Philippines. It is known for its distinctive blue and green shell and its sweet, tender meat, often used in Filipino dishes like crab fat

Common Name: Talangka

Scientific Name: Scylla serrata

Size: 10 cm - 15 cm

Weight: Up to 0.3 kg

Season: Year-round peaking in November to February

Market Price(kg): PHP 300 - 600

Talangka is commonly found in the coastal areas and mangrove regions of the Philippines, especially in provinces like Pampanga, Bataan, Batangas, and Cavite.

Talangka is a sought-after seafood in the Philippines due to its delicious, rich meat, which is often harvested from mud crabs living in estuaries and mangrove ecosystems. It is commonly prepared in a variety of traditional Filipino dishes, where its roe (aligue) is a delicacy. The commercial demand for talangka is high, particularly during special occasions and holidays, when its rich flavor makes it a popular choice for festive meals. In the seafood market, talangka is sold both live and cooked, and it plays a crucial role in the local economy, especially in regions where crab farming is a significant industry.
