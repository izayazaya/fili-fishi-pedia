Dalag-dagat

Common Name: Dalag-dagat

Scientific Name: Sillago sihama

Size: 20 cm - 30 cm

Weight: 0.2 kg - 0.4 kg

Season: Year-round peaking in November - May

Market Price(kg): PHP 200 - 300

Dalag Dagat, or Greater Sand Whiting, is found in shallow coastal waters, estuaries, and sandy-bottomed areas across the Philippines. It is particularly abundant in regions near Luzon, Visayas, and Mindanao, where sandy or muddy shores provide ideal habitats for this species.

Dalag Dagat (Sillago sihama) is a commercially significant marine fish in the Philippines, valued for its delicate, white flesh and mild taste. This fish is highly sought after in local markets and is a staple ingredient in Filipino seafood dishes such as fried, grilled, or stewed preparations. Its versatility and affordability make it a popular choice for households and restaurants alike. Fishermen commonly catch Dalag Dagat using nets and traps in shallow waters, and its year-round availability supports a steady supply. Efforts to manage sustainable fishing practices have been introduced to maintain healthy populations of this economically important species.
